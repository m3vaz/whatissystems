Courses by Topic

Systems Design Engineering program's core courses are organized into six main segments, which is displayed in each course's course code. Understanding these groupings is a great way to understand the main components of a Systems Design Engineering degree, and determine whether you think this program is right for you.

How course codes work

For a course SYDE XYZ

SYDE

Department Code

X

Year of Study the course is taken

Y

Represent the course topic group

Z

Term the course is taken in that year (odd = 1st term, even - 2nd term

General/Seminar Courses ( SYDE _0_ )

There one seminar course every term, and they are used to provide miscalleneous information which doesn't really fit into any other course. This includes information with regards to Administrative issues, co-op, school services, etc. Seminar time is usualyl quite structured in first and second year, with lots of important information to cover. In upper years, seminar time becomes more flexible in terms of content, with students usually deciding what they'd like to see, such as learning about grad school, bringing in past graduates, etc.

Mathematics Courses ( SYDE _1_ )

Being a technical program, all engineering disciplines have a strong emphasis on math. Many of the math courses you will take in the first three or so years are common across all engineering disciplines, however under their department-specific names. Engineering Mathematics courses focus more on teaching application of mathematical techniques, which allows you to understand the relevance and practicality of the concepts you are taught.

Computer Science Courses ( SYDE _2_ )

Knowledge in and experience with software development is crucial in today's high-tech world. Computer science courses within the SyDe curriculum are meant to familiarize you with basic concepts in software development, not to grow you into an experienced software developer.

Systems Engineering Courses ( SYDE _5_ )

Systems Engineering courses provide specific technical skills related to Systems Design Engineering. They ususalyly help explain how various different types of systems can be related to each other using math and physics concepts.

Design Courses ( SYDE _6_ )

As the program name might suggest, Systems Design engineer places strong emphasis on the design aspect of engineering. Courses cover topics such as design medothodoloy, psychology, economics, and ethics. There are three major design projects in 3rd and 4th year, which provide unparalleled opportunity to apply your work and academic experience towards an engineering project of your interest

Physics Courses ( SYDE _8_ )

Although SyDe Graduate students end up working in a variety of different industries, a strong understanding of the physical world around us is very important to be an effective engineer. As with the Mathematics courses, there is a strong emphasis on physics courses, and many of them are common across all engineering disciplines.
