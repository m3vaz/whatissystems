What is Systems Design Engineering?

The University of Waterloo's Systems Design Engineering program (also known as SYDE) is a prestigious, multidisciplinary program, offered as one of its kind in Canada. It has one of the highest employment rates for engineering cooperative education, as well as after graduation. Since it's inception in 1978, graduates have worked in fields including but not limited to engineering, such as finance, healthcare, environment, education, law, and consulting. SYDE has also bred entrepreneurs who have excelled in their industry of choice.

What sets Systems Design apart from the other engineering disciplines is really found in the name. Systems means we focus on engineering problems involving multiple engineering disciplines, whereas other disciplines will focus on the specific type of engineering problem in that field. Design means use a design-oriented approach to solve problems, by considering how engineering solutions affect the user, society, economy, and the environment.
